About

Sashika Nisansala

Sashika Nisansala Jayasumana, is born on September 28, 1981, in Deraniyagala, Sri Lanka. She is a renowned vocalist and producer known for her versatile talents in popsoul, rhythm and blues, and Indian classical music. Her journey in music began at Anula Vidyalaya in Nugegoda and further flourished through her education at the University of Kelaniya. With a career spanning over two decades since her debut in 1999, Sashika has established herself as a prominent solo singer, captivating audiences with her soulful vocals. She has received numerous accolades throughout her career, including State Awards, People's Awards, Raigam Awards, Popular Awards, Sumathi Awards, and more, showcasing her immense contribution to the music industry. As an independent artist, she continues to innovate and inspire, leaving an indelible mark on the world of music. Sashika is also a dedicated mother of two, balancing her passion for music with her commitment to family.

Career Journey

Sashika Nisansala Jayasumana's musical journey commenced at the tender age of 5 when she began playing the keyboard. Her talent quickly gained recognition, leading her to participate in prominent musical programs such as Muthuhara and Udara on television and radio platforms. Notably, she clinched victory in the Thurunu Shakthi singing competition organized by the National Youth Services Council, which served as a catalyst for her musical career. Following her triumph, she released her debut album, featuring hits like "Paramitha Nopuramu Api Dedena," "Thol Pethi Vitharak," "Sanda Reta Reta," and "Sanda Eliya Mamai Nam." With her captivating performances, Nisansala became a staple in outdoor musical shows, captivating audiences with her mesmerizing voice. In 2010, she ventured into the realm of music videos, showcasing her versatility as both a solo artist and playback singer. However, it was in 2021 that she released her first original music video, "Chandra Payanna," marking a significant milestone in her career and further solidifying her presence in the music industry.
